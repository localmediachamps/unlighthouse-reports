Why Move to Arlington?

The question isn't really why you should move to Arlington but why not? With top-rated schools, diverse neighborhoods, and an unparalleled sense of community, it's a place that offers something for everyone. Whether you're a young professional seeking vibrant nightlife and a short commute or a family looking for a safe, supportive environment for your kids, Arlington doesn't just meet these needs; it exceeds them.

  • Top-Rated Schools: Arlington Public Schools are a beacon of academic excellence and innovation, setting your children up for a bright future.
  • Neighborhood Variety: From the electric streets of Ballston and Clarendon to the serene, family-friendly vibe of Lyon Village, there's a perfect spot for everyone.
  • Commuting Ease: Say goodbye to long, stressful commutes. Arlington's efficient Metro system and pedestrian-friendly streets make getting around a breeze.
  • Culture and Community: The county's rich tapestry of art, festivals, and markets fosters a deep sense of belonging and engagement among its residents.

Exploring Arlington's Top Neighborhoods

Arlington, Virginia, is a mosaic of neighborhoods, each contributing its distinct color to the broader picture of this vibrant county. Whether you're drawn to the allure of bustling streets or the tranquility of leafy suburbs, Arlington's neighborhoods weave together the best of both worlds. 

Ballston & Clarendon

These neighborhoods are the lifeblood of Arlington's urban energy. Ballston & Clarendon are perfect for those who thrive in lively environments, offering an abundance of dining, nightlife, and shopping. The streets buzz with activity, drawing young professionals and those young at heart.

Rosslyn

Perched on the edge of the Potomac, Rosslyn offers sleek, modern living with breathtaking views of Washington, D.C. It's a hub for professionals who desire a polished, urban lifestyle, complete with upscale dining and easy access to both Arlington and the nation's capital.

Lyon Village, Lyon Park, Waverly Hills

These neighborhoods represent the essence of family-friendly living in Arlington. Lyon Village, Lyon Park, and Waverly Hills boast charming homes, verdant parks, and tight-knit communities that prioritize safety and camaraderie, making them ideal for families.

Shirlington

Known for its "urban village" charm, Shirlington is a delightful enclave within Arlington, offering a blend of cultural experiences with its theaters and libraries, alongside a welcoming, dog-friendly atmosphere. It's a pocket of tranquility and community within the bustling county.

Virginia Square

Virginia Square seamlessly blends educational institutions with residential living, making it a unique spot for students and academics. Home to both George Mason University's Arlington Campus and the Arlington Arts Center, it fosters a lively, intellectual community.

Crystal City

Now rebranded as National Landing, Crystal City is undergoing a transformation into a tech hub, thanks to Amazon's HQ2. It's becoming an even more desirable location for professionals in the technology sector, offering new housing developments and modern amenities.

Pentagon City

Adjacent to the Pentagon, Pentagon City is known for its shopping destinations like the Fashion Centre at Pentagon City. It offers a mix of luxury apartments and hotels, catering to both residents and visitors looking for convenience and accessibility.

Columbia Pike

Columbia Pike is one of Arlington's most diverse neighborhoods, offering a rich array of international cuisine, markets, and festivals. It's a community that celebrates diversity, with affordable housing options that attract a vibrant mix of residents.

Westover

Westover is an oasis of charm, featuring historic homes, tree-lined streets, and an iconic shopping area that harks back to a simpler time. It's known for its excellent schools and strong sense of community, making it a hidden gem within Arlington.

Bluemont

Bluemont is characterized by its suburban tranquility, expansive parks, and the popular W&OD Trail. It's a haven for outdoor enthusiasts and families looking for a peaceful neighborhood with easy access to Arlington's urban conveniences.

The Housing Market in Arlington

A testament to the area's allure, the housing selection presents a landscape that's as vibrant and diverse as the community itself. As you embark on the journey to find your new home in Arlington, the adventure is punctuated by the promise of discovering a place where life's chapters can unfold in comfort and style. 

Here's a deeper dive into what makes Arlington's housing market both exhilarating and unique:

Competitive Yet Rewarding

Arlington's housing market is indeed competitive, with demand often outpacing supply. Prices here are generally above the national average, a reflection of the high quality of life and the unmatched access to amenities — from top-tier schools and parks to shopping and dining options that rival those found in major cities. The investment in an Arlington home is not just in property but in a lifestyle enriched by convenience, culture, and community.

Diverse Housing Options

The architectural tapestry of Arlington's residential areas is rich and varied, offering something for every preference and stage of life:

  • Modern Condos and Apartments: For those who seek a maintenance-free lifestyle with amenities at their doorstep, Arlington's condos and apartments — especially in neighborhoods like Ballston, Clarendon, and Rosslyn — offer modern living spaces, often with breathtaking views and communal facilities such as gyms, pools, and security features.
  • Historic Homes: For history enthusiasts or those who cherish unique architectural details, areas like Maywood and parts of Lyon Village offer lovingly preserved homes that tell the tales of Arlington's past.
  • Single-Family Homes: Whether it's the spacious lawns of Waverly Hills or the inviting streets of Bluemont, families looking for room to grow will find single-family homes that blend suburban tranquility with urban accessibility.
  • Townhomes: A perfect middle ground, townhomes in neighborhoods like Shirlington and Westover provide the feel of a single-family home with less upkeep, often featuring multiple levels and private outdoor spaces.

Arlington’s Cost of Living Overview

Understanding the cost of living in Arlington, VA, is crucial for anyone considering a move to this dynamic area. The standard of living in Arlington is notably higher than many other parts of the United States, reflecting its desirability, quality of life, and the plethora of amenities and opportunities it offers.

Housing Costs

A significant factor in Arlington's cost of living is housing. Housing expenses in Arlington are considerably higher than the national average. Specifically, housing costs are 121% higher than the national average, with the median home price at about $997,420, and median rent for an apartment at approximately $3,033 per month​​. This stark difference underlines the competitive and high-demand nature of Arlington's real estate market.

Utilities and Transportation

When it comes to utilities, Arlington residents may find a small silver lining as utility prices are about 3% lower than the national average. However, transportation expenses, including bus fares and gas prices, are 9% higher than the national average, which could impact your monthly budget depending on your commuting habits and lifestyle​​.

Grocery and Everyday Expenses

Grocery prices in Arlington are 10% higher than the national average. This increase affects daily living costs, making budgeting for food and household supplies an important consideration for residents​​.

Overall Cost of Living

Overall, the cost of living in Arlington, VA, is 41% higher than the national average. This encompasses a broad range of expenses from housing to groceries, utilities, and transportation​​. The high cost of living index in Arlington (164.3) confirms that living in this area is 64.3% higher than the U.S. average and also highlights the quality and high standard of living that residents enjoy​​.

Employment Opportunities in Arlington and the D.C. Area

Pursuing a career in Arlington, VA, and the surrounding D.C. area means engaging with an employment landscape marked by diversity, opportunity, and growth. Whether your expertise lies in government, technology, or professional services, Arlington offers a dynamic and supportive environment for your career journey. 

  • Government and Defense: Leveraging its geographic proximity to Washington, D.C., Arlington is a central hub for government-related jobs, including positions at the Pentagon and numerous federal agencies. This sector remains a staple of the area's employment offerings, providing stable and rewarding career paths.
  • Tech Hub: The technology sector in Arlington is on a rapid ascent, fueled by the arrival of giants like Amazon's HQ2 and a constellation of innovative startups. This burgeoning tech scene is creating a multitude of opportunities for professionals in software development, cybersecurity, and IT support, among others​​.
  • Professional Services: Arlington also boasts a robust market for professional services. Consulting firms, legal practices, and nonprofit organizations offer ample opportunities for career advancement, making it an ideal locale for those in the consulting, legal, and charitable sectors​​.
  • Education and Higher Learning: Arlington stands out for its commitment to education, from its highly regarded public schools to a variety of private institutions offering diverse educational philosophies. The proximity to esteemed higher education institutions like Georgetown and George Mason University further enhances the appeal for families and individuals alike​​.
  • Healthcare and Biotechnology: This region is home to a growing healthcare sector, including world-class medical facilities and research institutions. Biotechnology firms, leveraging the area's strong foundation in scientific research, offer career paths in research, development, and healthcare technology.
  • Hospitality and Tourism: Given its rich historical significance and proximity to the nation's capital, Arlington enjoys a vibrant tourism industry. Jobs in hospitality management, event planning, and cultural institutions abound, catering to the millions of visitors the area attracts each year.

Tips for Planning Your Move to Arlington

Transitioning to Arlington is an opportunity to immerse yourself in a vibrant, connected community. It's about finding the right balance between the excitement of urban life and the serene embrace of suburban comfort. Here are key aspects to consider for a smooth move:

  • Transportation: Arlington's public transit system is top-notch, making commuting and getting around a breeze. Take advantage and familiarize yourself with the efficient transportation options available.
  • Lifestyle Perks: The area is known for its vibrant shopping, dining, and parks. Dive into the leisure activities that make Arlington's lifestyle unique and fulfilling.
  • Community Engagement: Getting involved in local events and festivals is a great way to feel connected. Arlington offers a variety of activities that help new residents become part of the community.
  • Partner with Local Experts: The housing market here is competitive, but local experts can give you an edge. Realtors and movers provide valuable insights, helping you find the perfect home that fits your lifestyle.
  • Understanding Market Dynamics: Arlington's housing market varies by neighborhood, influenced by factors like Metro proximity and new developments. Stay informed and flexible to navigate the market effectively.
  • The Importance of Preparation: Preparation is crucial in Arlington's fast-paced housing market. Make sure your finances are ready, know what you're looking for, and be prepared to act quickly to secure your new home.
  • Choosing a Mover: Opt for a reputable moving company, such as Reputation Movers, for a stress-free move. They're known for their reliability and customized service, ensuring your transition is smooth.
